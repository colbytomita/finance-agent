5. How A Finance Agent Should Use This Universe

Use the universe as a catalyst radar, not a buy/sell list. Every alert should be classified as primary fact, credible secondary report, social/rumor, or PM judgment before it enters a thesis or risk decision.

  • Start with primary sources. For companies use IR, EDGAR, exchange notices, and court/regulator filings. For macro use Fed, BLS, BEA, Treasury, EIA, ECB/BOJ/PBOC, OPEC, and official calendars.
  • Score every hit. Assign impact 1-5, confidence high/medium/low, and actionability. Upgrade low-confidence/high-impact items for human review rather than trading them automatically.
  • Separate cause from prediction. A short report, FDA decision, or FOMC statement may cause a move. A strategist, analyst, or newsletter may only anticipate one. Treat these differently in backtests.
  • Use cross-source confirmation. Pair social or press alerts with filings, official statements, on-chain data, price/volume, options, borrow, and peer read-throughs.
  • Build watchlists by exposure. Map each catalyst to tickers, ETFs, suppliers, customers, peers, debt, commodities, and currencies. Many catalysts first appear in one venue and move another asset.
  • Track false positives. Store alerts that did not move markets, activist campaigns that failed, product launches that were priced in, and rumors that were denied. This improves future reliability scores.
  • Refresh time-sensitive dates. Earnings, PDUFA, court hearings, index events, merger votes, lockups, macro releases, and conferences can change. Never convert an inferred window into a hard date.
  • Turn high-impact events into work items. For each top catalyst, define the model line affected, confirm/disconfirm signal, prep owner, post-event update, and add/hold/trim/exit or hedge implication.
